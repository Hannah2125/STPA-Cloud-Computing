Manned Vehicles


1. X-15 rocket plane ( 1959-1968)- One of the first space vehicles, the X-15 rocket plane, included a narrow fuselagewith sides containing fuel anad an intial computerized system. The simple aircraft was the main goal to leade people into space. About 200 flightswere made between 1959 and 1968. The information that was gathered was dynmaic to both the space race and the creation of the Spaceshuttle.

2.Project Gemini (1962-1966, manned missions began from 1965)- The target for Project Gemini was to handle experiments and to establish practice techniques for lunar missions. Gemini 3, the first Gemini flight,was launched on March 23,1965. While 9 other missions followed. The missions collected information about weightlessness on humans and the first space walk.





Unmanned Vehicles


1.Mars Rover- On June 10 and July 7, 2003, NASA launched twin robot exploration rovers to Mars. They landed on January 3rd and 24th, 2004. They were searching for answers about the history of water on Mars. Their main goal was to look for rocks and soils for clues about post water activity. Some instruments the Rover carried were Panoramic Cameras, Miniature Thermal Emission Spectrometers, Mossbauer Spectrometer, Alpha Particle X-Ray Spectrometer, magnets to collect magnetic dust particles, and the Rock Abrasion Tool.

2.Pioneer 10 (March 3,1972)- Launched on March 3rd, 1972, Pioneer 10 completed the first mission to the planet Jupiter. With 3 stages for launch sequence, Pioneer 10 reached a speed of 52,000 km/hr, on the last stage to reach Jupiter. That made Pioneer 10 the fastest man made object to leave Earth.
